Business Strategies

A business strategy is the combination of all the decisions taken and actions performed by the business to accomplish business goals and to secure a competitive position in the market. It is the backbone of the business as it is the roadmap which leads to the desired goals.

Our Ideas

Communicate transparently with your customers

We are all in this together, so the ideal way is to stay transparent about what your business is going through with your consumers. Customers can empathize with companies facing a crisis, as long as the communication is transparent. Communicating with customers to make them understand their perception of the product/solution offered by you.

Maintaining healthy relationship with contracted parties

During the lockdown it is understandable that it might be difficult to pay vendors/suppliers. However, to give your suppliers, landlords’, vendors etc. sufficient notice would be helpful in case there is going to be any delay in payments, so they can also be prepared and there is no bitterness in this already difficult time.

Managing employees & related optimization

Primarily to try to retain the people who can at-least afford to lose their jobs employed should be to cut the salaries of the highly paid employees for large companies considering layoffs. However, when the time comes to make the tough call, do it with compassion look out for government directives, and offer extra compensation as and when required. Such implementation should be carried out only once and not in stages such that it does not affect employee morale.

Keep Your Team Engaged

Keep your team updated about every development as they depend on you. As a promoter, it is your responsibility to keep your team members engaged and stay connected with them through video conferencing tools like Zoom and Google Hangouts. To maintain high spirits within the team and understand the general mood within your remote workforce is of extreme importance.

Communication to stakeholders

Consult with your investors or external experts to plan the right form of communication with stakeholders, most importantly customers and employees. An honest conversation on the situation and its impact on your business should be had with your entire leadership team. Utmost empathy along with transparent reasons should be delivered to any negative message.

Speed matters

We all know that it will not be enough for companies to recover revenues gradually as the crisis starts to decrease. They will need to rethink their revenue profile fundamentally, to position themselves for the long term and to get ahead of the competition. To do this companies must SHAPE up and start rethiking their strategies

Start-up mindset

This favors action mainly over research, and testing mainly over analysis. Establish a brisk cadence to encourage agility and accountability: daily team check-ins, once a week half an hour CEO review, and once in 2 months 1 hour reviews.

Human at the core

Companies will need to rethink their operating model based on how their people work best and most efficiently. more than half of the businesses surveyed by McKinsey in early April said that their new remote sales models were proving nearly as much as (29 percent) or more effective (31 percent) than traditional channels.

Acceleration of digital, tech, and analytics. It’s already a cliché

It’s already a cliché that the COVID-19 crisis has entirely shifted the work medium to digital. But the best companies are going one step ahead , by enhancing and expanding their digital channels. They’re successfully using advanced analytics to combine new sources of data.

Purpose-driven customer playbook

Companies need to understand what exactly customers will value, post-COVID-19, and develop new use cases and tailored experiences based on those insights , this will not only help the business to grow, but also gain popularity
